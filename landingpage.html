Water conservation is essential for everyone.
Here are some useful facts and simple suggestions that will help you understand more about water.

Latest Facts

· There's as much water in the world today as there was thousands of years ago. The water from your faucet could contain molecules that dinosaurs once drank!
· Nearly 97% of the world's water is salty or otherwise undrinkable. Another 2% is locked in ice caps and glaciers. That leaves just 1% for all agricultural, manufacturing, community, and personal household needs.
· We drink very little of our drinking water. Less than 1% of the treated water produced by water utilities is actually consumed. The rest goes on lawns, washing machines, and down toilets and drains.

Household Water Conservation Tips

Fix Leaks: Leaky faucets and toilets waste thousands of gallons per year. Regularly check and repair leaks.
Efficient Fixtures: Install low-flow showerheads, faucets, and dual-flush toilets. These can reduce water use by up to 50%..
Shorter Showers: Aim for 5-minute showers instead of long, hot baths..

MoreInnovative Technologies and Tools

Water-Efficient Appliances: Dishwashers and washing machines with high-efficiency ratings can save water and energy.
Leak Detection Systems: Modern systems can monitor water usage and detect leaks early.
Water Recycling Systems: Some homes now feature systems that recycle greywater for non-potable uses like irrigation.
